JAK1 inhibitor efficacy in rheumatoid arthritis Phase II study

Abstract: This study evaluates SN-2847, a selective JAK1 inhibitor, in patients with Rheumatoid Arthritis.
The JAK-STAT pathway is critically involved in inflammatory signaling. PD-L1 and CRP were measured as biomarkers.

Results: Compound SN-2847 showed IC50 of 12nM against JAK1. hERG IC50 was within acceptable limits.
LogP was 2.4. Phase II results demonstrated significant efficacy versus placebo.

The EGFR Signaling pathway was assessed as off-target. GSE198234 omics data supports JAK1 as a validated target.
ICH M7(R2) compliance was verified for mutagenic impurities.

Conclusion: SN-2847 represents a promising lead for Rheumatoid Arthritis with favorable ADMET profile.
